Composite products: import risk categories from 31 January 2024

Published 7 December 2023

Applies to England, Scotland and Wales

  • Publication for Northern Ireland

This page outlines the risk categories for composite products, which are foodstuffs intended for human consumption that contain both processed products of animal origin (POAO) and plant products as a main ingredient – not just added for flavouring or processing. Plant-based products with no POAO content are not composite products.  

You can use the information on this page to find the right risk category for your import.  

1. How to find the risk category for this classification  

You can refer to the general principles of risk categorisation for composite products (see Section 4) or use the table of individual risk categories (see Section 5) to check the risk category for individual commodities.  

1.1  General principles of risk categorisation 

Section 4 includes general principles for risk categorisation for the relevant subclassifications – for example, milk and milk products. This will give you an explanation of the principles that inform the risk categories for those commodities.  

1.2  The information in the table

For each commodity, the table includes:

  • the Trade Tariff commodity code for the product 

  • the corresponding IPAFFS code (this is the code you will enter into the Import of products, animals, food and feed system (IPAFFS) when you are ready to import)  

  • the description of the commodity  

  • the risk category 

2.  How to search this page  

If you want to search the information on this page, press Ctrl+F on your keyboard. This will bring up a small search box where you can enter in the word or phrase you are looking for. The program will then search through the entire document displaying any instances of the word or phrase you entered.  

For example, if you knew your Trade Tariff commodity code was 102211000, you would press Ctrl + F, enter 102211000 into the search box, and then press return. This will take you to the relevant row in the table, where you can check the risk category.  

2.1 Find your Trade Tariff commodity code

You can use the Trade Tariff tool to find the commodity code for your import.

3.  Updates to risk categories  

Risk factors will be reviewed regularly. Commodities may be moved into higher or lower TOM risk categories in future to reflect changing levels of risk.  

You’ll be notified in advance if there is a change to the import risk category of a commodity you have imported.  

4. General principles of risk categorisation for composite products  

From 31 January 2024, all composite products imported from the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland and Liechtenstein, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) will be either:  

  • in the low import risk category (some products will have a further risk assessment and may become medium risk, but not before April 2024)  

  • not placed in an import risk category because it is exempt from official import controls.  

Find out if your product is exempt from import controls.
