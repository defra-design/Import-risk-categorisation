Details

This guidance applies to businesses in Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) that plan to import animals or animal products from the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) from 31 January 2024. It is based on information set out by the Border Target Operating Model (BTOM).

Check these pages if you are looking for risk categories for:

  • Animals and animal products imported from non-EU countries
  • Plants and plant products imported from the EU to Great Britain
  • Plants and plant products imported from non-EU countries to Great Britain

1. What you need to do

From 31 January 2024, you need to follow these steps.

1.1 Find the correct risk level

The first step is for you to find the correct risk level for your import. These are divided into the following classifications:

  • Live animals and germinal products: live aquatic animals, which includes live fish and animals for ornamental, aquaculture and re-stocking purposes; bees; germinal products (semen, ova and embryos). Live aquatic animals imported for human consumption (for example, crabs, lobster or live bivalve molluscs) are categorised as products of animal origin (POAO)
  • Products of animal origin (POAO), which are goods derived from animals and fit for human consumption: dairy products and milk; egg and egg products; fishery products and bivalve molluscs; gelatine, collagen and highly refined products; honey and apiculture products; and meat, meat preparations and meat products
  • Animal byproducts (ABPs), which are goods derived from animals but not fit for human consumption: animal protein; blood and blood products; fats; gelatine and collagen; hides and skins; horns and trophies; milk products; pet food and products for the manufacture of pet food; phosphate products; wool, hair and feathers
  • Composite products, wwhich are foodstuffs intended for human consumption that contain both processed products of animal origin (POAO) and)plant products as a main ingredient – not just added for flavouring or processing. (Plant-based products with no POAO content are not composite products.

1.2 Read the risk category rules

There are different rules that you need to follow for low risk, medium risk and high risk commodities.

The risk category rules will explain what you need to do, depending on the category of risk you have identified.  

1.3 Enter the relevant risk category into IPAFFS

Once you have found the risk category and followed the relevant rules, you will need to enter the risk category into IPAFFS, the Import of products, animals, food and feed system.

2. Get help

If you have any questions about notifying imports of animal products or which BTOM risk category your product is in, email the APHA imports team at imports@apha.gov.uk.
