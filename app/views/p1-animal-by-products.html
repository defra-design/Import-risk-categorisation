This page outlines the risk categories for animal byproducts, which are goods derived from animals but not fit for human consumption. These are divided into subclassifications (see sections 5-15) – for example, animal protein, blood and blood products, and gelatine and collagen.  

You can use the information on this page to find the right risk category for your import.  

1. How to find the risk category for this classification  

You can refer to the general principles of risk categorisation listed under each subheading or use the tables of individual risk categories to check the risk category for individual commodities.  

1.1  General principles of risk categorisation 

Sections 5-15 include general principles for risk categorisation for the relevant subclassifications – for example, milk and milk products. This will give you an explanation of the principles that inform the risk categories for those commodities.  

1.2  The information in the tables

Each table lists:  

  • the Trade Tariff commodity code for the product 

  • the corresponding IPAFFS code (his is the code you will enter into the Import of products, animals, food and feed system (IPAFFS) when you are ready to import)  

  • the description of the commodity  

  • the risk category 

2.  How to search the information on this page  

If you want to search the information on this page, press Ctrl+F on your keyboard. This will bring up a small search box where you can enter in the word or phrase you are looking for. The program will then search through the entire document displaying any instances of the word or phrase you entered.  

For example, if you knew your Trade Tariff commodity code was 2835260000, you would press Ctrl + F, enter 2835260000 into the search box, and then press return. This will take you to the relevant row in the table, where you can check the risk category.  

2.1  How to find your commodity code

You can use the Trade Tariff tool to find the commodity code for your import.

3.  Updates to risk categories  

Risk factors will be reviewed regularly. Commodities may be moved into higher or lower TOM risk categories in future to reflect changing levels of risk.  

You’ll be notified in advance if there is a change to the import risk category of a commodity you have imported.  

4.  Definitions  

4.1  Channelling  

Channelling describes the requirement that an ABP consignment must be moved directly from the point of entry to the approved point of destination. You must send confirmation of arrival to the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA).  

4.2  Shelf-stable at ambient temperature

This means it can be safely stored at room temperature or ‘on the shelf’.

5.  Animal protein  

5.1  General principles  

  • Hydrolysed protein for use outside the feed chain is categorised as low risk  

  • Hydrolysed protein for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk  

  • Processed animal protein (terrestrial) not for human consumption, for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk  

  • Processed animal protein (aquatic) not for human consumption, for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk  

  • Animal protein (insects) not for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain is categorised as low risk  

  • Processed animal protein (insects) not for human consumption, for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk  

  • Processed animal protein not for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain is categorised as medium risk

15. Other animal by-products (ABPs)  

15.1  General principles  

  • ABP outside the feed chain or trade samples are categorised as medium risk. Animal by-products included in this category are set out in Import Information Note ABP/8a  

  • Apiculture products, for use in apiculture, are categorised as medium risk  

  • Egg products for use in animal feed are categorised as medium risk  

  • Egg products for use outside the feed chain are categorised as low risk  

  • Feeder rodents or lagomorphs and feeder chicks are categorised as medium risk  

  • Fish oil for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk  

  • Fish oil for use outside the feed chain is categorised as low risk  

  • Hay and straw are categorised as medium risk  

  • Highly processed intermediate products and ABPs for pharmaceutical, cosmetic, or laboratory reagent use, are categorised as low risk. (The definition of a ‘highly processed’ product is being finalised. Defra will publish guidance on this definition in late 2023.)  

  • Manure – processed is categorised as low risk
