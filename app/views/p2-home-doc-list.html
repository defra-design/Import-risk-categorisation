P2. Check import risk categories and related rules for animals and animal products imported from the EU, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain from 31 January 2024

In this guide, you will find the Border Target Operating Model (BTOM) risk categories and rules for animals and animal products imported from the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales). These rules will apply to goods imported from 31 January 2024.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Animal and Plant Health Agency

Applies to England, Scotland and Wales

Details

This guidance applies to businesses in Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) that plan to import animals or animal products from the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) from 31 January 2024. It is based on information set out by the Border Target Operating Model (BTOM).

Check these pages if you are looking for risk categories for:   

  • Animals and animal products imported from non-EU countries

 1. What does this apply to?

From 31 January 2024, you will need to assess the risk category of these imports:

1.1 Live animals and germinal products

This classification includes live animals and their germinal products (semen, ova and embryos); live fish and animals for ornamental, aquaculture and re-stocking purposes; bees. 

Live aquatic animals imported for human consumption (for example, crabs, lobster or live bivalve molluscs) are categorised as products of animal origin (POAO).   

1.2 Products of animal origin (POAO)   

These are goods derived from animals and fit for human consumption, including: dairy products and milk; egg and egg products; fishery products and bivalve molluscs; gelatine, collagen and highly refined products; honey and apiculture products; and meat, meat preparations and meat products.

1.3 Animal byproducts (ABPs)   

These are goods derived from animals and not fit for human consumption, including: animal protein; blood and blood products; fats; gelatine and collagen; hides and skins; horns and trophies; milk products; pet food and products for the manufacture of pet food; phosphate products; and wool, hair and feathers.

1.4 Composite products   

These are foodstuffs intended for human consumption that contain both processed products of animal origin (POAO) and plant products as a main ingredient – not just added for flavouring or processing. (Plant-based products with no POAO content are not composite products.)   

 2. What you need to do    

From 31 January 2024, you need to follow these steps.   

2.1 Find the correct risk level for your commodity   

You can do this in 2 ways:   

  • You can read the general principles for our classifications. This page gives you a summary of how different types of animals and animal products will be categorised for risk.    

  • You can download a copy of our spreadsheet, which lists risk categories for individual commodities.   

If the spreadsheet does not give you the risk category for the commodity you’re importing or you don’t understand it, refer to the general principles for our classifications for more information about how that type of commodity will be categorised.    

  2.2 Read the risk category rules    

There are different rules that you need to follow for low risk, medium risk and high risk commodities.    

The risk category rules will explain what you need to do, depending on the category of risk you have identified.    

3. Get help    

If you have any questions about risk categories or how to import animals or animal products from 31 January 2024, email the APHA imports team at imports@apha.gov.uk.
