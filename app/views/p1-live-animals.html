This page outlines the risk categories for live animals, aquatics and germinal products. You can use it to find the risk category for your commodity. 

1.  How to find the risk category for this classification  

You can refer to the general principles of risk categorisation (see Section 4) or use the table of individual risk categories (see Section 5) to check the risk category for individual commodities.  

1.1  General principles of risk categorisation 

Section 4 outlines the general principles for risk categorisation for this classification of import – namely, live animals, aquatics and germinal products.  

1.2  The information in the table

For each commodity, the table lists: 

  • the Trade Tariff commodity code for the product

  • the corresponding IPAFFS code (this is the code you will enter into IPAFFS, the Import of products, animals, food and feed system when you are ready to import) 

  • the description of the commodity  

  • the risk category  

2.  How to search this page  

If you want to search the information on this page, press Ctrl+F on your keyboard. This will bring up a small search box where you can enter in the word or phrase you are looking for. The program will then search through the entire document displaying any instances of the word or phrase you entered.  

For example, if you knew your Trade Tariff commodity code was 102211000, you would press Ctrl + F, enter 102211000 into the search box, and then press return. This will take you to the relevant row in the table, where you can check the risk category.  

You can use the Trade Tariff tool to find the commodity code for your import.

3.  Updates to risk categories  

Risk factors will be reviewed regularly. Commodities may be moved into higher or lower TOM risk categories in future to reflect changing levels of risk.

You’ll be notified in advance if there is a change to the import risk category of a commodity you have imported.  

4.  General principles of risk categorisation for live animals and germinal products  

Live animals and germinal products (semen, ova and embryos) are categorised as high risk.   

In addition to live mammals, this classification includes:  

  • live aquatic animals, which includes live fish and animals for ornamental, aquaculture and re-stocking purposes, and their germinal products  

  • bees  

  • germinal products (semen, ova and embryos), including hatching eggs  

Live aquatic animals imported for human consumption (for example, crabs, lobster or live bivalve molluscs) are categorised as products of animal origin (POAO).
