General principles of import risk categorisation, from 31 January 2024

Published 11 December 2023

Applies to England, Scotland and Wales

This page explains the principles informing risk categories for different types of animals and animal products, from 31 January 2024. These are relevant to businesses (England, Scotland and Wales) that plan to import these commodities from the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales). These principles are set out by the Border Target Operating Model (BTOM).   

1. About risk categories 

The information on this page is divided into the following classifications:  

  • Live animals and germinal products  

  • Products of animal origin  

  • Composite products  

  • Animal byproducts 

1.1 Updates to risk categories  

Risk factors will be reviewed regularly. Commodities may be moved into higher or lower BTOM risk categories in future to reflect changing levels of risk.  

You’ll be notified in advance if there is a change to the import risk category of a commodity you have imported.  

2. Definitions  

2.1 ‘Non-raw’ dairy products   

For the purposes of the BTOM, ‘non-raw’ dairy products are products containing milk that have been subject to pasteurisation or an equivalent or higher level of treatment (for example, UHT processing).   

2.2 Fishery products   

‘Fishery products’ are defined in Annex I of retained EU regulation 853/2004 and include:   

  • fin fish and their products (excluding live fish)   

  • aquatic animals (for example, crabs and lobsters) or their products, fit for human consumption and imported as products of animal origin (including live or viable aquatic animals)   

2.3 Category II   

References to Category II relate to Annex I of Retained EU Regulation 2019/2120.   

2.4 Channelling   

Channelling describes the requirement that an ABP consignment must be moved directly from the point of entry to the approved point of destination. You must send confirmation of arrival to the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA).   

3. Live animals and germinal products: general principles of risk categorisation  

Live animals and germinal products (semen, ova and embryos) are categorised as high risk.   

In addition to live mammals, this classification includes:   

  • live aquatic animals, which includes live fish and animals for ornamental, aquaculture and re-stocking purposes, and their germinal products   

  • bees   

  • germinal products (semen, ova and embryos), including hatching eggs   

Live aquatic animals imported for human consumption (for example, crabs, lobster or live bivalve molluscs) are categorised as products of animal origin (POAO).  

4. Products of animal origin (POAO): general principles of risk categorisation  

4.1 All POAO   

A POAO commodity will be in the low BTOM risk category if it meets all of the following criteria:   

  • It is shelf-stable at ambient temperature, which means it can be safely stored at room temperature or ‘on the shelf’   

  • It has undergone a treatment step or other processing in its manufacture (for example, canning) to eliminate or reduce micro-organisms of concern to an acceptable level   

  • It is clearly identified as intended for human consumption   

  • It is securely packaged or sealed in visibly clean containers   

  • It is labelled in English and accompanied by a commercial document providing information on the nature of the commodity, the quantity and number of packages, the country of origin, the manufacturer and the ingredients   

  • It is not infant formula or follow-on formula, baby food, or food for special medical purposes   

4.2 Dairy products and milk   

  • Milk for human consumption is categorised as medium risk   

  • Dairy products and colostrum-based products for human consumption, preserved at frozen or chilled temperatures (5.   • The following commodities have been placed in the low BTOM risk category from January 2024, pending further risk assessments:   

  • Non-raw dairy products (see 2.1) and colostrum-based products for human consumption, which are preserved at frozen or chilled temperatures (Category II (see 2.3))   

  • Dairy products and colostrum-based products for human consumption, other than those mentioned in Category II (see 2.3)   

Any changes to these BTOM risk categories will not take place before April 2024. We will communicate with traders well in advance of any changes to commodity categorisation.   

4.3 Eggs and egg products   

  • Eggs for human consumption are categorised as medium   

  • Egg products for human consumption which are preserved at frozen or chilled temperatures (Category II (see 2.3)) are categorised as medium   

  • Egg products for human consumption, other than those mentioned in Category II (see 2.3), are categorised as low   

4.4 Fishery products and bivalve molluscs   

 a. Criteria for wild caught fishery products   

A wild caught fishery product will be in the low risk category if it meets the following criteria:   

  • It is not live or would be unable to survive if returned to the environment (‘not viable’)   

  • It comes from an approved establishment and is identified accordingly   

  • It has not been subject to processing, as defined in Article 2 of Retained EU Regulation 852/2004 – for example, by heating, curing, smoking, maturing, drying, marinating, extraction, extrusion or a combination of these   

  • It is accompanied by the required illegal, unreported and unregulated (IUU) documents, for example, a valid catch certificate and proof of storage and processing, where applicable   

  • It is not fish that is associated with histamine (for example, scombroids), which would be categorised as medium risk   

If it does not meet all these criteria, the fish will be in one of the other ‘fishery products’ commodity types and categorised as medium risk.   

 b. Fishery products and unprocessed products   

Fishery products that have undergone an operation affecting their anatomical wholeness (for example, gutting, heading, slicing, filleting or chopping) and otherwise meet the definition of an ‘unprocessed product’ outlined in Article 2 of Retained EU Regulation 852/2004 (including if they have been chilled or frozen), may still be classed as wild caught if the other criteria listed above apply.   

 c. General principles for risk categorisation    

  • Fishery products, other than those mentioned in Category II (see 2.3), and other than wild caught fishery products, are categorised as medium   

  • Fishery products from aquaculture and bivalve molluscs for human consumption, which are not in hermetically sealed containers intended to render them stable at ambient temperature (Category II (see 2.3)), are categorised as medium   

  • Wild caught fishery products are categorised as low (if they meet the listed criteria)   

  • Bivalve molluscs (live or processed) are treated separately for the purposes of the BTOM and will be medium risk in all cases.   

4.5 Gelatine, collagen and highly refined products   

  • Gelatine and collagen are categorised as low   

  • Highly refined products for human consumption are categorised as low risk  

4.6 Honey and apiculture products   

  • Apiculture products for human consumption are categorised as medium   

  • Honey is categorised as low risk  

4.7 Meat, meat preparations and meat products   

  • Meat other than meat mentioned in Category II (see 2.3), and meat products derived from such meat, for human consumption is categorised as medium   

  • Minced meat, mechanically separated meat and meat preparations for human consumption (Category II (see 2.3)) is categorised as medium   

  • Poultry meat and poultry meat products for human consumption is categorised as medium   

  • Rabbit meat, game meat, and their meat products for human consumption is categorised as medium   

  • Rendered animal fat and greaves for human consumption is categorised as low   

4.8 Other POAO   

  • Infant formula, follow-on formula, food for special medical purposes (FSMP) and baby food: categorised as Medium, regardless of product type, due to the vulnerability of the intended consumers, are categorised as medium risk   

5. Composite products: general principles of risk categorisation 

From 31 January 2024, all composite products imported from the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland and Liechtenstein, the Faroe Islands and Greenland to Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) will be either:   

  • in the low import risk category (some products will have a further risk assessment and may become medium risk, but not before April 2024)   

  • not placed in an import risk category because it is exempt from official import controls.   

Find out if your product is exempt from import controls.  

6. Animal byproducts: general principles of risk categorisation  

6.1 Animal protein   

  • Hydrolysed protein for use outside the feed chain is categorised as low risk   

  • Hydrolysed protein for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk   

  • Processed animal protein (terrestrial) not for human consumption, for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk   

  • Processed animal protein (aquatic) not for human consumption, for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk   

  • Animal protein (insects) not for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain is categorised as low risk   

  • Processed animal protein (insects) not for human consumption, for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk   

  • Processed animal protein not for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain is categorised as medium risk   

6.2 Blood and blood products   

  • Treated blood, blood products, for use in animal feed are categorised as medium risk   

  • Treated blood, blood products from equine, for use outside the feed chain are categorised as low risk   

  • Untreated blood, blood products from equine, for use outside the feed chain are categorised as medium risk   

  • Treated bloods excluding equine, for use outside the feed chains are categorised as low risk   

  • Untreated blood, blood products excluding equine, for use outside the feed chain are categorised as medium risk   

6.3 Fats   

  • Fat derivatives for use outside the feed chain are categorised as low. Channelling (see 2.4) will be required.   

  • Fat derivatives for use in animal feed are categorised as medium   

  • Rendered fats not for human consumption, for use in animal feed, are categorised as medium risk   

  • Rendered fats not for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain, are categorised as medium risk. Channelling (see 2.4) will be required   

6.4  Gelatine and collagen   

  • Gelatine or collagen not intended for human consumption, for use in animal feed, are categorised as medium risk   

  • Gelatine or collagen not intended for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain, are categorised as low risk. Channelling (see 2.4) is required for photogelatine (gelatine for use in the photographic industry).  

6.5 Hides and skins   

  • Treated hides and skins (excluding items that are not subject to any import restrictions) are categorised as low risk   

  • Untreated hides and skins are categorised as medium risk   

6.6 Milk products   

  • Milk and milk-based products not for human consumption, for animal feed use, are categorised as medium risk   

  • Milk and milk-based products not for human consumption, for use outside the feed chain, are categorised as low risk   

6.7 Horns and trophies   

  • Horn product for organic fertilisers is categorised as low risk. Channelling (see 2.4) is required.   

  • Treated game trophies (excluding items that are not subject to any import restrictions) are categorised as low risk. Items not subject to any import restrictions are set out in Import Information Note ABP/6c.   

6.8 Pet food and products for the manufacture of pet food   

  • ABP for the manufacture of pet food are categorised as medium risk   

  • Dog chews are categorised as medium risk   

  • Flavouring innards are categorised as medium risk   

  • Pet food – canned is categorised as low risk   

  • Pet food – processed (other than canned) is categorised as low risk   

  • Pet food – raw (including if frozen) is categorised as medium risk   

6.9 Phosphate products   

  • Organic dicalcium phosphate and tricalcium phosphate, for use in animal feed, are categorised as medium risk   

  • Organic dicalcium phosphate and tricalcium phosphate, for use outside the feed chain, are categorised as low risk   

6.10 Wool, hair and feathers   

  • Pig bristle is categorised as medium risk   

  • Treated feathers are categorised as low risk. Import conditions are set out in Import Information Note ABP/22b   

  • Dry untreated wool or hair are categorised as low risk. However, they are only low risk if they are both securely enclosed in packaging and sent directly to a plant producing derived products for uses outside the feed chain or a plant carrying out intermediate operations, under conditions which prevent the spreading of pathogenic agents.   

  • Other permitted untreated wool or hair are categorised as medium risk   

  • Wool grease or fatty substances derived therefrom (including lanolin and lanolin alcohol), for use outside the feed chain are categorised as low risk. However, processed products derived from wool grease that no longer require further processing when they arrive in Great Britain (other than mixing, coating, assembling or packaging before placing on the market), and are intended for the manufacture of cosmetic products, are subject to specific categorisation. This will be confirmed when the commodity code spreadsheet for non-EU imports is published in due course.   

6.11 Other animal by-products (ABPs)   

  • ABP outside the feed chain or trade samples are categorised as medium risk. Animal by-products included in this category are set out in Import Information Note ABP/8a   

  • Apiculture products, for use in apiculture, are categorised as medium risk   

  • Egg products for use in animal feed are categorised as medium risk   

  • Egg products for use outside the feed chain are categorised as low risk   

  • Feeder rodents or lagomorphs and feeder chicks are categorised as medium risk   

  • Fish oil for use in animal feed is categorised as medium risk   

  • Fish oil for use outside the feed chain is categorised as low risk   

  • Hay and straw are categorised as medium risk   

  • Highly processed intermediate products and ABPs for pharmaceutical, cosmetic, or laboratory reagent use, are categorised as low risk. (The definition of a ‘highly processed’ product is being finalised. Defra will publish guidance on this definition in late 2023.)   

  • Manure – processed is categorised as low risk  

7. What to do once you find the risk category   

Follow the import risk category rules relating to the risk category.     

8. Get help  

If you have any questions about notifying imports of animal products or which risk category your product is in, email the APHA imports team at imports@apha.gov.uk.
